History of Corn

  • Corn first started to be domesticated at what is estimated to be seven thousand years ago in South and Central America
  • The intial plant it was domesticated was much smaller and had fewer kernals, spaced further apart
  • Over thousands of years, it slowly became the corn we know today
  • Maize (as a crop) spread south along the coast of Peru, and North to higher up in North America
  • As Indians spread out (and some migrated to what is now the US) they brought maize along with them
  • Corn became a major part of the natives diets
  • Christopher Columbus brought corn to Europe from the first time after reaching the new world
  • It is believed that corn was served at the first Thanksgiving
  • Corn has now become the most grown crop in the America's
  • Just as corn has changed over time, so has its uses. Over 40% of corn is now used for ethanol
